Thursday's easy, Friday's traditional, Saturday's modern shared plates. Plus the Sunday lunch send-off and the Loulé market mid-trip.

The three dinners

Book ahead for Fri & Sat.

Casa do Pescador and Salmora both fill up on weekends. Thursday's Tico Tico is walk-in only.

Casa do Pescador
Friday night
Behind Vilamoura marina · ~€30–40 each · Traditional Portuguese
Friday's dinner. Traditional seafood and steak in a hidden square. Famous for cataplana - the copper-pot fish stew for two. Book ahead. Generous portions, great wine list.
Full details
Salmora Live Kitchen
Saturday night
Just off marina · ~€30–40 each · Modern Portuguese tapas
Saturday's dinner. Modern tapas, open kitchen, social bar seating. Order 6–8 small plates between you. Book ahead for Saturday - it fills up fast before the night starts.
Full details
Akvavit
Alternative
Marina-front · ~€35–45 each · Scandi-Portuguese since 1990
Iconic marina-front restaurant. Swedish-Portuguese fusion with a marina-deck terrace. Swap in for either Friday or Saturday if the first choice is full - book at least a day ahead.
Full details
Quarteira backstreets

Thursday dinner & Sunday lunch.

The backstreets a few rows behind the seafront have the best-value Portuguese cooking. No booking needed.

🍽
Restaurante Tico Tico
Thursday night
Quarteira backstreets · ~€15–25 each · Walk-in
Family-run, traditional Portuguese. Thursday's pick. Ask what the prato do dia is - always the best value. Bread and olives arrive at the table automatically (couvert charge - say you don't want it if so).

Sailor's Corner
Lunch spot
Quarteira seafront · ~€12–18 each · Walk-in
Thursday lunch with Monty and the Sunday send-off. Lighter food, great for a final Sagres in the sun before the airport. Simple, no fuss, reliable.

Marisqueira Sol e Mar
Local fish
Quarteira · Price per kilo - ask first
Local fish restaurant in the backstreets. Priced by the kilo - always ask the price before you order. Authentically Portuguese, no frills, very fresh fish.
