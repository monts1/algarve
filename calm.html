Friday's calm night.

Pick one tasting - port at Oporto Tavern or wine at Rolha a Rolha. No need to do both. Then a proper Portuguese dinner with a bottle.

The tasting

Port or wine - pick one.

Two very different atmospheres. Oporto Tavern is bar-side and informal; Rolha a Rolha is a sit-down structured tasting. Both great.

Oporto Tavern
Port option
Vilamoura marina · 17:00–02:00 · €25–35 each
Bruno and the team line up 3–5 ports at the bar - they walk you through each one and pair with tapas. Informal, relaxed, great for people who don't take wine too seriously.
Full details Maps
Rolha a Rolha
Wine option
Quarteira, 5 min from apartment · €25–30 each
Structured two-hour tasting of 7 Portuguese wines, walked through by region and variety. More formal than Oporto Tavern - good for wine learning. Book a day or two ahead.

After the tasting

Friday dinner.

Book one of these ahead for Friday evening. Both work well after the tasting window.

Casa do Pescador
Friday pick
Behind Vilamoura marina · Mains €15–25 · Traditional Portuguese
Traditional seafood and steak in a hidden square behind the marina. Famous for cataplana - a copper-pot fish stew for two. Generous portions, proper Portuguese wine list.

Akvavit
Alternative
Vilamoura marina-front · Mains €18–29 · Scandi-Portuguese
Iconic marina-front restaurant since 1990. Swedish-Portuguese fusion with a marina-deck terrace. Good backup if Casa do Pescador is full - book at least a day ahead either way.
