Cerro da Vila Roman Ruins.

A real Roman villa - mosaics, fish-salting tanks, small museum. Walking distance from Vilamoura marina. Closed Saturday and Sunday. Friday morning is the only window this trip.

A proper Roman site - not reconstructed, not a theme park. The villa dates from the 1st to 4th century AD and the mosaics are remarkably well-preserved under protective covers. The fish-salting tanks are the standout oddity - this was industrial-scale Roman food production right on the coast.

Walking distance from the Vilamoura marina (about 15 minutes). Small attached museum gives good context about the site. An audio guide is available at the entrance - worth taking for the extra detail it adds to the fish tanks and bath complex.

The Friday morning slot (11:00) slots in perfectly between breakfast at the apartment and a marina lunch, before the afternoon rest and the calm evening ahead.
