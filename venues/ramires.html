Ramires.

The original Algarve frango piri-piri. Family-run in Guia since 1964, three generations on. A 12-minute taxi out of town - worth the run for the bird, the chips, the piri-piri sauce, and the noise.

Guia is the village that put frango piri-piri on the map, and Ramires is its inventor - same family, same recipe, since 1964. Half a chicken, a pile of fries, a salad, the secret sauce in a little jug on the table. That's the order.

Get an Uber out - about 12 minutes from Quarteira, €10-12 each way. No need to book - it's huge inside and they move fast. Solid backup if you fancy something different to the marina or fancy backstreets. Bring an appetite and tissues.

Good to know

  • The order: half a chicken, chips, salad, secret piri-piri sauce on the side
  • It's the original - the rival across the road (Frango do Guia) is the breakaway family
  • The sauce is the thing - house piri-piri made fresh, lethal in small amounts
  • Tissue-and-fingers situation - they don't pretend it's anything else
  • Big rooms, noisy, fast service - good energy
  • Pre-book the Uber back for after - taxi rank can be slow late on
