Praia da Falésia.

Iconic Algarve orange cliffs with pine trees above and beach below. A gentle clifftop path with viewpoints all the way along. 60–90 minutes there-and-back from Vilamoura marina.

One of the Algarve's most photographed natural features. Dramatic orange and red sandstone cliffs with pine forest above and a long, wide beach below. The clifftop path runs for several kilometres with viewpoints at intervals - you don't need to do all of it, just as far as feels right.

Start near the eastern end of Praia da Rocha Baixinha, near the Vilamoura marina end. The path is well-worn and clearly marked. Hour out, hour back at a relaxed pace. The beach below is accessible by wooden staircases at several points if you want to go down.

Best done in the morning before it gets too hot in May. Takes about 2–3 hours in total if you include time to stop and look.

Good to know

  • Cliff edges crumble - stay back from the lip at all times
  • Take water and sunscreen - there's little shade on the path itself
  • Best in morning light; cooler and better photography before midday
  • Wooden staircases down to the beach at several points if you want to descend
  • Trainers are completely fine - no hiking boots needed
  • Works well as a Thursday afternoon or Sunday morning option if you have time
