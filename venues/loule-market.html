Loulé Saturday Market.

The famous Moorish red-domed market hall with farmers' and gypsy markets spilling around it on Saturdays. Live music inside. Petiscos at the stalls for lunch. Bus 9 from Quarteira, 25 min.

Loulé's Moorish red-domed market hall is one of the most distinctive buildings in the Algarve. On Saturdays it's surrounded by a farmers' market and a larger gypsy market that spills through the surrounding streets - genuinely local, not tourist-facing.

Inside the hall: fish, meat, produce, flowers, and usually live music on Saturday mornings. The stalls spilling outside sell everything from grilled sausages and prawns to the folhado de Loulé pastry (a local speciality, order one).

Combine with Loulé Castle (5-min walk from the market) and a wander through the old town before the bus back. Get there by 10:00 - the best stalls start packing up around noon.

Good to know

  • Get there by 10:00 - best stalls start packing up around noon
  • Buy the folhado de Loulé pastry - it's a local speciality and hard to find elsewhere
  • Petiscos lunch at the stalls: grilled sausages, prawns, grilled corn - budget €10–15 each
  • Loulé Castle is 5 min walk from the market - combine it while you're there
  • Bus 9 from Quarteira bus station - buy via Vamus app (or pay on board)
  • Return buses are less frequent after noon - check the timetable before you go
