Restaurante Tico Tico.

Family-run, traditional Portuguese in the Quarteira backstreets. Thursday's dinner pick. No booking needed - just walk in and ask for whatever the prato do dia is.

The kind of place that's been feeding the neighbourhood for years. Family-run, traditional Portuguese cooking - no theme, no concept, just proper food at honest prices. The Quarteira backstreets a few rows behind the seafront have several of these; Tico Tico is the pick for Thursday's first dinner together.

Ask what the prato do dia (daily special) is when you sit down - it's always the best value and usually features whatever came in fresh that day. Expect something like grilled fish with rice and salad, or a slow-cooked meat dish.

Bread and olives will arrive at the table automatically - this is the couvert and it's charged. Say "Não quero o couvert, obrigado" if you'd rather save the €2–3 each. Cash may be preferred - worth checking before you order.
